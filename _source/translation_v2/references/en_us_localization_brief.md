Target: publishable US English technical-editorial prose.

This is localization, not sentence transport. The translated text must read as if the same author had written it directly in English for a technical audience.

Method:
- Preserve meaning, voice, argument structure, caveats, and humor.
- Rebuild sentence movement and connective logic when literal transfer from Portuguese sounds translated.
- Prefer the wording an English-language technical writer would naturally publish, even when that requires recasting the sentence.

Register:
- Serious, readable, technically precise prose.
- Not bureaucratic.
- Not literal Portuguese with English words.

Common failure patterns to avoid:
- Portuguese-influenced clause order
- overly formal or bureaucratic register
- inconsistent terminology or borrowed Portuguese phrasing
- prose that remains understandable but does not sound authored in English
